About Me

Hi!
I'm Dahye Kim,
currently a Computer Engineering student at the University of Waterloo.
To talk about my past and the reason why I chose computer engineering,
I came to Canada by myself when I was 16 to let myself to learn and experience the world.
One day when I was in a library,
I came across a book that inspired me to choose computer engineering as my future career pathway.
The book was a report written by the UN which predicted what may be in store in the future.
They envision that most surgeries will be done by robots,
portable 3D scanners will be used widely to produce materials
which people can scan and print it out directly,
everyone will enjoy virtual reality in daily life,
and most importantly, computers will be a core of everything.
These may sound like something everyone knows nowadays,
but I read this book few years ago and at that time,
I didn't know what 3D printers are and virtual reality game is.
And couldn't even imagine doctors being replaced by robots.
While reading the book, I saw the new world and it made me realize that
there are going to be pivotal changes in the future and it made my heart beat.
So I would like to be a pioneer of the technologically advanced era
and that is why I am determined to become an engineer;
to make the impossible possible and make the dreams a reality in the world.
I can already see virtual reality is becoming familiar to many people,
and also can see 3D printers pretty often around me.
The world is changing very rapidly,
I realized I need to go a step ahead of the world,
so I am and will always be trying my best to learn more,
and perform the best to achieve my dream sooner than I expect.

To talk about things I enjoy, I enjoy baking, cooking, making miniatures and anything with my hands,
also like trying new hobbies,
I have tried making clothes, cosmetics, and soap.
I also love board games, poker, escape rooms, so basically things require strategy.
